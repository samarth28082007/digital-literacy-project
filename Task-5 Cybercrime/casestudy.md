Topic-Cyberbullying
Cyberbullying is when people use the internet to hurt or scare someone on purpose. They do this on media, messaging apps and other online places. Cyberbullying is different from bullying because it can happen at any time and lots of people can see it, which makes it really bad.

Usually it starts when someone posts something online. Then someone else might make a comment or joke about it. This can get worse and worse until they are saying hurtful things spreading rumors or even making fake profiles to bother the person. The fact that people can be anonymous on the internet makes them feel like they can be meaner. After a while the person being bullied might get left out of groups or feel really embarrassed because of mean posts and messages.

Cyberbullying usually happens to teenagers and young adults who use media a lot. People who are a little different, shy or do not have a lot of friends are more likely to get bullied.

The bad things that can happen because of cyberbullying are very serious. People who get bullied can feel stressed, anxious and sad. They might not do well in school anymore. In bad cases they might stop talking to people or even hurt themselves. Also the people doing the bullying might get in trouble at school or even with the law.

This shows how important it is to be kind and responsible when we're online. We need to know what cyberbullying is and stop it from happening so we can keep people safe, on the internet. Cyberbullying is a problem and we need to do something about cyberbullying to prevent cyberbullying.
